COPPA (Children Online Privacy Protection Act)

Parents are put in control of the collection of personal information from children under 13. The Federal Trade Commission, the nation’s consumer protection agency, enforces the COPPA Rule, which states what operations websites and online services should do to protect children’s privacy and safety online. Furthermore, we do not advertise to children under 13.

Fair Information Practices

To be in line with the Fair Information Practices law, we’ll take the following actions as soon as a data breach occurs.

We will notify the user via email regarding the same within one business day. We also agree to the individual redress principle, which requires that every individual has a right to pursue legally enforceable rights against data collectors and processors who fail to follow the law itself. Individuals also have all the resources to trigger a legal investigation against data processors.

CAN-SPAM Act

The CAN-SPAM Act is a law that establishes guidelines for sending commercial emails and allows the users to stop the emails which are being sent to them. Failure on doing so is considered a violation and penalties may apply.

To be following CAN-SPAM we agree to the following:

We never send misleading subjects through our emails.

We include the physical address of our office headquarters.

In case of an opt-out/Do Not Track signal, we make sure, the user does not receive emails from us at all.

We attach a link at the end of every email from where the user can unsubscribe.
